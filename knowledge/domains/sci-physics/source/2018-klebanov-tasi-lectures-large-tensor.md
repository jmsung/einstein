# TASI Lectures on Large $N$ Tensor Models

**Authors:** I. Klebanov, F. Popov, G. Tarnopolsky  ·  **Year:** 2018  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1808.09434

## One-line
Pedagogical review comparing the three "basic" large-$N$ limits (vector, matrix, tensor) and developing the tensor-model machinery dominated by melonic diagrams, with applications to SYK-like quantum mechanics.

## Key claim
For $O(N)^3$-symmetric tensor models with a "tetrahedral" quartic interaction $g\,\psi_{abc}\psi_{ab'c'}\psi_{a'bc'}\psi_{a'b'c}$, the large-$N$ limit (with $g^2 N^3$ fixed) is dominated by melonic Feynman diagrams — giving an SYK-like solvable theory *without* quenched disorder. The three basic limits are distinguished combinatorially by the snail-vs-melon competition: vectors → snails win (bubble chains); matrices → draw (planar); tensors → melons win.

## Method
Combinatorial Feynman-diagram analysis in $d=0$ scalar integrals as warm-up, then Schwinger–Dyson equations for the 2-point and 4-point functions in the melonic-dominated large-$N$ limit. Auxiliary-field ($\sigma$) saddle-point for vector models; double-line / 't Hooft topology for matrices; tetrahedral / prismatic index contractions for tensors. Anomalous dimensions found by solving $g_S(h)=1$, $g_A(h)=1$ where the kernel eigenvalue is built from the conformal IR propagator $G(t)\propto |t|^{-1/2}\,\mathrm{sgn}(t)$.

## Result
Vector $O(N)$ large-$N$ free energy has susceptibility exponent $\gamma=1/2$ (branched-polymer universality), with closed form $f_0(\lambda)=\sum_k \frac{(-\lambda)^k}{4^k(k+1)6^k}\binom{2k}{k}$ (finite radius of convergence). Tensor models with $N^3$ Majorana fermions and tetrahedral Hamiltonian reproduce SYK 2-point scaling $\Delta_\psi=1/4$; bipartite complex variant gives operator spectrum $h\to n+1/2$ with first values $h=2,2,3.77,4.26,5.68,6.34,\ldots$ — and a *complex* dimension $h\approx 1/2+1.5251i$ for the $\bar\psi\psi$ operator, below the BF bound $m_{BF}^2=-1/4$ (instability). The $O(N)^3$ sextic "prismatic" scalar model (Giombi–Klebanov–Popov–Prakash–Tarnopolsky) has a real-coupling fixed point for $N>53$ with $g_1\sim N^{-3}$ — a candidate stable bosonic large-$N$ CFT. Number of $SO(N)^3$-invariant $2k$-particle operators grows as $k!\,2^k$ ⇒ Hagedorn temperature vanishes in large $N$.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. The paper is about high-energy theory (holography, SYK, AdS/CFT) — none of the 23 Einstein Arena problems is in this domain. Faint methodological resonance: large-$N$ saddle-point / Schwinger–Dyson and the snail-vs-melon combinatorial accounting are examples of "the right limit makes a hard problem tractable," which mirrors the wiki's parameterization-selection / LP-duality framings, but the techniques don't transfer to sphere packing, autocorrelation, or kissing-number directly.

## Open questions / connections
- Are there stable purely bosonic melonic CFTs in $d>1$ beyond the prismatic candidate, or do complex scaling dimensions $d/2+i\alpha(d)$ always reappear?
- The $k!\,2^k$ operator counting → vanishing Hagedorn $T$: does this combinatorial growth law have an analog in the contact-graph / configuration-counting problems on arena (P11, P15)?
- Extends colored tensor models (Gurau et al.) and connects to SYK (Sachdev–Ye, Kitaev); leaves open whether tensor models give a quantum-gravity dual richer than the SYK Schwarzian sector.
