# Notes on tensor models and tensor field theories

**Authors:** R. Gurau  ·  **Year:** 2019  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.03531

## One-line
Pedagogical lecture notes on random tensor models and tensor field theories, presenting their melonic large-$N$ limit and the resulting class of analytically tractable conformal field theories.

## Method
Combines several techniques: (i) conformal partial wave / shadow formalism to invert four-point functions and extract operator dimensions via $k(\Delta,J)=1$; (ii) Schwinger–Dyson equations closed by the melonic truncation $\Sigma(x) = \frac{\lambda^2}{(q-1)!}G(x)^{q-1}$; (iii) 1/N expansion of colored tensor / vector-tensor / $O(N)^3$ models via graph degree (jackets); (iv) 2PI effective action and Wilsonian renormalization group with anomalous dimension $\eta_k = -\tfrac{1}{2}k\partial_k\ln Z_k$.

## Result
Establishes that melonic CFTs admit closed-form solution of the conformal SDE: in $d$ dimensions with $q$-body interaction, $\Delta_\phi = d/q$ at the IR fixed point, with operator spectrum determined by poles of $1/(1-k(\Delta,J))$. The melonic large-$N$ limit is shown to be richer than vector (gives anomalous dimensions) but simpler than planar matrix (resummable). Explicit graph-degree formula $\omega(\tilde G) = 3 + \sum_q(q/4 - 1)F_q(\tilde G)$ classifies leading CTKT graphs as melon-tadpoles (Proposition 7).

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. This is QFT/random-geometry technology — the Einstein Arena problems are extremal combinatorial / packing / autocorrelation problems, which use LP/SDP duality, Cohn-Elkies, and discrete optimization rather than $1/N$ field theory. The conformal partial wave + Schwinger-Dyson machinery here is orthogonal to the agent's current toolbox.

## Open questions / connections
- Step 3 of the large-$N$ program (resummation / bounding the remainder) remains unsolved outside $d=0$.
- Connection to SYK model without quenched disorder — alternative route to melonic dynamics.
- Higher-dimensional tensor field theories: classification of new melonic CFTs and their stability (line of fixed points in bosonic tensor model, ref. [98]).
