# Lessons from the Ramond sector

**Authors:** Nathan Benjamin, Ying-Hsuan Lin  ·  **Year:** 2020  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.02394

## One-line
Sharpens the (1+1)d fermionic modular bootstrap by enforcing Ramond-sector consistency and bosonization/Sugawara constraints, ruling out putative "kink" CFTs and producing stronger universal bounds.

## Key claim
Requiring Ramond-sector integrality/unitarity and consistency of the GSO-bosonized partition function eliminates several previously-reported extremal modular functions; in $N=(1,1)$ SCFTs, a relevant deformation must exist for $\tfrac{3}{2}<c<10.6$, and a SUSY-breaking relevant deformation for $\tfrac{3}{2}<c<10.3$.

## Method
Combine modular invariance under $\Gamma_\theta$, $\Gamma_0(2)$, $\Gamma^0(2)$ for the four spin-structure partition functions $(Z_{NS\pm},Z_{R\pm})$ with the bosonization/fermionization square (tensoring by the Arf SPT $(-1)^{\mathrm{Arf}}$). Diagnostics: (i) $S$-transform NS+ to obtain R+ and check integer, unitary ground-state degeneracies; (ii) Sugawara decomposition $T=T_s+T_o$ bounds central charge and spin-2 current counts given $n_J$ spin-1 currents. Numerical RNS bootstrap via SDPB with reduced characters extracts bounds on operator gaps.

## Result
Extremal NS+ functions at $c=9$ and $c=33/2$ from Bae–Lee–Song have non-integer R-sector degeneracies (e.g. ground-state count $5336132768/231$ at $c=33/2$) and violate $h,\bar h\ge c/24$ — hence unphysical. The $N=1$ Maloney–Witten partition function has continuous R spectrum with degeneracy $+16$ at the threshold; a minimal positivity-restoring combination (vacuum sum + 2 scalar primaries + 8 copies of $c=3/2$ free theory) gives R ground-state degeneracy 32. Sugawara envelope: $c\ge(1+\sqrt{1+8n_J})/4$ for $n_J\ge 300$, so $c/\sqrt{n_J}\ge 1/\sqrt 2$. A flavored extremal modular function at $c=3$ from Dyer–Fitzpatrick–Xin is identified as a specific fermionic CFT via Sugawara on its bosonization.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. Relevant only as methodology — the Sugawara lower bound $c\ge(1+\sqrt{1+8n_J})/4$ is a clean example of "fixed quantum-number → minimum dimension" bound analogous to kissing-number / sphere-packing LP bounds, and the bootstrap-with-extra-consistency-check pattern parallels the triple-verify rule.

## Open questions / connections
- Can Sugawara-style lower bounds be extended to higher-spin (W-algebra) conserved currents, given the lack of a systematic W-algebra classification?
- Can a supersymmetric theory with odd Witten index be built from non-degenerate $N=1$ super-Virasoro characters with non-negative coefficients?
- Does incorporating the Sugawara $c$–$n_J$ bound into modular bootstrap LP/SDP tighten existing bounds on conserved-current counts?
