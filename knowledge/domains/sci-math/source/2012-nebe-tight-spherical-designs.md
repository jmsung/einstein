# On tight spherical designs

**Authors:** G. Nebe, B. Venkov  ·  **Year:** 2012  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1201.1830

## One-line
Uses arithmetic of quadratic forms on the lattice generated by a putative tight spherical $t$-design to rule out infinitely many candidate dimensions for the two remaining open cases $t=5$ and $t=7$.

## Key claim
For tight 7-designs (which can only exist in dimension $n = 3d^2-4$): if $d$ is odd and $d \not\equiv \pm 1 \pmod{16}$, then $d \equiv 3 \pmod{32}$ or $d \equiv -3 \pmod{32}$ (with constraints on $\det(\Lambda)$); together with even-$d$ exclusions (Theorem 3.3), this kills $d \in \{4,5,7,8,9,11,12,13,16,19,20,21,\ldots\}$, including the previously-open $m=6$ case for tight 5-designs.

## Method
Treats the design $X$ as generating an integral lattice $\Lambda$, with dual $\Gamma=\Lambda^*$. From the design equations $(D2),(D4),(D6)$ for moments $\sum_{x\in X}(x,\alpha)^k$ and integrality of inner products $(x,\alpha)\in\mathbb{Z}$, derives congruences mod 120 and 36 (Lemmas 2.1, 2.2) on $(\alpha,\alpha)$ and $(\alpha,\beta)$. Combines $p$-adic valuation analysis (Corollary 3.2), characteristic vectors of odd unimodular lattices, and Milgram–Braun Gauss-sum identities on the discriminant group $\Gamma^*/\Gamma$ to obtain contradictions modulo small primes.

## Result
Theorem 3.3: no tight 7-design when $v_2(d)\in\{2,3,4\}$ (under mild prime conditions on $d^3-d$). Theorem 3.5: for odd $d$ with $d\not\equiv\pm 1\pmod{16}$, only $d\equiv \pm 3\pmod{32}$ survive. Theorem 4.6 / Corollary 4.7: tight 5-designs ruled out for $m\in\{3,4,6,10,12,22,28,30,34,42,46,\ldots\}$ (dimension $n=(2m+1)^2-2$). Only known tight 7-designs remain at $d=2,3$; only known tight 5-designs at $m=1,2$.

## Why it matters here
General background — tight spherical $t$-designs are extremal configurations on $S^{n-1}$ that simultaneously optimize moments of low-degree polynomials, relevant to packing/code-design framings (Delsarte LP, Cohn–Elkies). Lattice-theoretic non-existence techniques here (characteristic vectors, Gauss sums, $p$-adic integrality of moment identities) are reusable for any Arena problem where a candidate optimum is forced to generate an integral lattice with constrained discriminant.

## Open questions / connections
- Tight 5-design at $m=6$ (dimension $n=167$) is excluded here; $m=5,7,8,9,\ldots$ status remains mixed — which are settled by these methods vs. open?
- Tight 7-design at $d=6$ (dimension $n=104$) still open — even $d$ with $v_2(d)=1$ not covered.
- Extends Bannai–Munemasa–Venkov [4] (2004); does the characteristic-vector $\pmod{32}$ argument generalize to other lattice-rigidity problems (kissing configurations, universally optimal codes)?
