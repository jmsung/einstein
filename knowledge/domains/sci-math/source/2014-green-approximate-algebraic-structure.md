# Approximate algebraic structure

## One-line
ICM 2014 survey on the robustness regime of two inequalities ‚Äî Young's convolution inequality and the Gowers norm inequalities ‚Äî where near-equality forces approximate-algebraic structure (approximate groups, nilsequences).

## Key claim
Near-extremizers of these inequalities are *structured*: sets $A$ with $\|f*f\|_2^2 \geq K^{-1}\|f\|_2^4$ (equivalently multiplicative energy $E(A) \geq |A|^3/K$) are controlled by coset nilprogressions (Breuillard‚ÄìGreen‚ÄìTao structure theorem); functions with $\|f\|_{U^{s+1}[N]} \geq K^{-1}$ correlate with an $s$-step nilsequence (Green‚ÄìTao‚ÄìZiegler inverse theorem).

## Method
Stability/robustness framework: characterize equality cases (subgroups for Young; polynomial phases for Gowers norms), then ask which $f$ achieve $v(f) \geq M/K$. Tools: Balog‚ÄìSzemer√©di‚ÄìGowers, Pl√ºnnecke‚ÄìRuzsa, Hrushovski's Lie model theorem + Hilbert's 5th problem (Gleason lemmas), Croot‚ÄìSisask almost-periodicity, Sanders' quasi-polynomial Bogolyubov, nilmanifold equidistribution, generalised von Neumann inequalities.

## Result
(i) Every $K$-approximate group is $K'$-controlled by a coset nilprogression of rank/class bounded in $K$ (Theorem 2.2). (ii) Helfgott: a $K$-approximate subgroup of $\mathrm{PSL}_2(\mathbb{F}_p)$ has $|A| \leq K^{C}$ or $\geq K^{-C}|G|$. (iii) Bourgain‚ÄìGamburd expansion: random walks on $\mathrm{PSL}_2(\mathbb{F}_p)$ equidistribute in $O(\log|G|)$ steps. (iv) Inverse $U^{s+1}[N]$ theorem + M√∂bius-nilsequences orthogonality $\Rightarrow$ asymptotics for linear configurations in primes.
