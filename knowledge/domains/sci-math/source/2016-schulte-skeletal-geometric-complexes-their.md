# Skeletal Geometric Complexes and Their Symmetries

**Authors:** E. Schulte, A. I. Weiss  ·  **Year:** 2016  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1610.02619

## One-line
Survey of the classification of skeletal regular and chiral polyhedra and regular polygonal complexes in Euclidean 3-space $E^3$ under the Grünbaum–Dress framework, where faces may be skew or helical and the focus is on edge-graph + face structure rather than solid membranes.

## Key claim
In $E^3$ there are exactly **48 regular polyhedra** (Grünbaum–Dress: 18 finite + 30 apeirohedra split into 6 planar + 12 blended + 12 pure), **6 infinite families of chiral polyhedra** (3 finite-skew-faced with rational parameter $a/b$ or $c/d$; 3 helix-faced with real parameter), and exactly **73 regular polygonal complexes** (48 polyhedra + 4 non-simply-flag-transitive 2-skeletons of regular 4-apeirotopes + 21 simply-flag-transitive non-polyhedral complexes).

## Method
Group-theoretic Wythoff-style construction: each regular polyhedron is generated by 3 involutions $R_0,R_1,R_2$ satisfying Coxeter relations $R_i^2=(R_0R_1)^p=(R_1R_2)^q=(R_0R_2)^2=1$; chiral polyhedra by 2 generators $S_1,S_2$ with $S_1^p=S_2^q=(S_1S_2)^2=1$. Classification proceeds by enumerating mirror vectors $(d_0,d_1,d_2)\in\{(2,1,2),(1,1,2),(1,2,1),(1,1,1)\}$ and the associated crystallographic special group (one of $\{3,3\},\{3,4\},\{4,3\}$). Edge graphs are matched to crystallographic nets (pcu, fcu, bcu, dia, nbo).

## Result
The pure regular apeirohedra factor into a $4\times 3$ table (mirror vector $\times$ Platonic special group). Chiral finite-faced polyhedra $P(a,b), Q(c,d), Q(c,d)^*$ are combinatorially chiral and combinatorially non-isomorphic for distinct $\pm(a,b),\pm(b,a)$ (resp. $\pm(c,d),\pm(-c,d)$). Chiral helix-faced polyhedra are combinatorially isomorphic to the regular helix-faced polyhedron in the same family and deform continuously into it; they also cover the corresponding Platonic solid. Only 5 crystallographic nets (pcu, fcu, bcu, dia, nbo) arise as edge graphs of the 21 simply-flag-transitive non-polyhedral regular complexes.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. The skew-polygon / helical-face viewpoint and the cubic-lattice ($\Lambda_1,\Lambda_2,\Lambda_3$) constructions give vocabulary for highly symmetric 3-periodic point/edge configurations that could inform discrete-geometry packing problems (kissing/contact graphs in $E^3$), but no quantitative bound transfers directly.

## Open questions / connections
- No intuitive combinatorial invariant is known to distinguish the infinitely many isomorphism classes of finite-faced chiral polyhedra $P(a,b)$ as $a/b$ varies — open detection problem.
- Extends Grünbaum (1977) + Dress (1981, 1985) regular-polyhedra enumeration and Pellicer–Schulte (2010, 2013) polygonal-complex enumeration; ties to McMullen's forthcoming monograph on higher-dimensional skeletal polytopes.
- Why exactly these 5 nets (pcu, fcu, bcu, dia, nbo) realize all 21 simply-flag-transitive complexes — structural reason for the small count.
