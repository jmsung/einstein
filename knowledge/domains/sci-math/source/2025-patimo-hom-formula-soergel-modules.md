# A Hom formula for Soergel modules

**Authors:** L. Patimo  ·  **Year:** 2025  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.06161

## One-line
Constructs a "cohomology submodule" $H_w \subset B_w$ inside indecomposable Soergel bimodules for arbitrary Coxeter groups, proves a Hom formula matching the Hecke-algebra pairing, and exhibits explicit counterexamples to a 2007 conjecture of Soergel.

## Key claim
For any Coxeter group $(W,S)$ with a GKM-realization, every indecomposable Soergel bimodule $B_w$ contains a distinguished left-$R$-free submodule $H_w$ with basis $\{P_x\}_{x \leq w}$ and graded rank $v^{-\ell(w)} \sum_{x \leq w} v^{2\ell(x)}$; moreover $k \otimes_R \mathrm{Hom}^\bullet_{\mathrm{SBim}}(B,B') \cong \mathrm{Hom}^\bullet_{\overline{Z}}(\overline{B},\overline{B'})$ with graded dimension equal to the Hecke pairing $([B],[B'])$ — but this fails when $\overline{Z}$ is replaced by $R$ alone.

## Method
Two equivalent constructions: (i) Fiebig's moment-graph / structure algebra $Z$ realizes $H_w$ as the cyclic $Z$-module generated by $1^{\otimes}$; (ii) Libedinsky's light leaves basis realizes $H_w$ as the orthogonal complement (under the intersection form on $BS(w)$) of light leaves whose $U/D$-decoration contains at least one $D$. Proposition 3.15 identifies them; the Hom formula is then proved by adjunction reducing to $B' = R$ and a support-filtration / $P_x$-annihilator argument.

## Result
Theorem A establishes $H_w$'s graded rank, giving top-heaviness of Bruhat intervals for arbitrary Coxeter groups (extending Björner–Ekedahl beyond the crystallographic case) and a Hodge-theoretic proof of Carrell–Peterson's KL-triviality criterion. Theorem B is Soergel's Hom formula for Soergel modules (the $\overline{Z}$-module version). Two explicit counterexamples to the $R$-only version: (a) in affine $\widetilde{A}_2$, $w = stutst$ gives a $\overline{B_w}$ decomposable as a right $R$-module via $\widetilde{\mathrm{Fl}}_w \cong G/B \times \mathrm{Gr}_{stu}$; (b) in the rank-3 universal Coxeter group, $w = stustu$ admits an explicit degree-2 element $\bar{b} \in \overline{BS(w)}$ giving a right-$R$-map $R \to \overline{B_w}$ not induced by any bimodule map (verified by SageMath).

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie — this is pure representation theory (Soergel bimodules, Hecke categories, KL theory) and does not inform the optimizer / packing / autocorrelation problems on Einstein Arena.

## Open questions / connections
- Extends Björner–Ekedahl top-heaviness from crystallographic to arbitrary Coxeter groups — possible structural-combinatorics tool if a problem ever touches Bruhat-order shape.
- Refutes Soergel's [Soe07, Remark 6.8] conjecture that homogeneous degree-$2\ell(y)$ elements $c_y$ playing the role of $P_y$ in $R \otimes R$ exist for infinite Coxeter groups.
- Singular-Soergel-bimodule analogue sketched in Remark 3.18 (extends to $B_w^I$ via singular light leaves [EKLP24]).
