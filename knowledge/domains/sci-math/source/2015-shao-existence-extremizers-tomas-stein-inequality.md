# On existence of extremizers for the Tomas-Stein inequality for $S^1$

**Authors:** Shuanglin Shao  ·  **Year:** 2015  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1507.04302

## One-line
Proves that the adjoint Fourier restriction inequality $\|\widehat{f\sigma}\|_{L^6(\mathbb{R}^2)} \le \mathbf{R}\|f\|_{L^2(S^1)}$ on the unit circle admits an extremizer, and that every extremizer is antipodally symmetric in modulus.

## Key claim
**Theorem 1.2:** an extremal function exists for the Tomas-Stein inequality on $S^1$. **Theorem 1.3:** every extremizer satisfies $|f(-x)| = |f(x)|$ a.e. on $S^1$. The argument rests on the strict comparison $\mathbf{R} > (5/2)^{1/6}\mathbf{R}_P$ between the sphere and paraboloid sharp constants (Proposition 2.1).

## Method
Concentration-compactness adapted to a nonlocal operator: (i) strict comparison $\mathbf{R} > (5/2)^{1/6}\mathbf{R}_P$ via a perturbation expansion ($\partial_\varepsilon|_{\varepsilon=0}\Psi = 11/16 > 0$) of a Gaussian ansatz cap-pasted onto the sphere; (ii) antipodal symmetrization using a 20-orbit critical-point analysis on the convolution functional $\|\widehat{f\sigma}\|_6^6$; (iii) a refined Tomas-Stein inequality via bilinear transverse restriction estimates and Bourgain–Moyua–Vargas–Vega-style cap decomposition; (iv) profile decomposition that approximates $\widehat{f\sigma}$ on small caps by linear Schrödinger waves, ruling out concentration to a Dirac mass.

## Result
Existence of an $L^2(S^1)$ extremizer is established, with the explicit modulus symmetry $|f(-x)|=|f(x)|$. The quantitative gap $\partial_\varepsilon\Psi(0) = 11/16$ encodes the strict separation between sphere and paraboloid sharp constants. No closed-form extremizer is exhibited (contrast: Foschi 2015 shows constants are extremizers for $S^2$); the work leaves the $S^1$ extremizer characterization open.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. Relevant if any arena problem reduces to sharp adjoint-restriction / Strichartz constants on a curve, or if concentration-compactness + profile decomposition framing is needed for an extremal-existence question (e.g., uncertainty-principle or autocorrelation problems where Gaussian ansatze appear).

## Open questions / connections
- Explicit form of the $S^1$ extremizer — Foschi-style Cauchy-Schwarz arguments (effective on $S^2$) have not produced an analog for $S^1$.
- Extends Christ–Shao 2012 ($S^2$) and Kunze 2003 / Foschi 2007 / Hundertmark–Zharnitsky 2006 (paraboloid Strichartz) to the $S^1$ endpoint case.
- High-dimensional sphere extremizer existence remains open: argument fails because the strict sharp-constant comparison and the even-integer-exponent algebra both break down.
