# Low-Complexity Tilings of the Plane

**Authors:** J. Kari  ·  **Year:** 2019  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.04183

## One-line
A short review of an algebraic (commutative-algebra) method for proving periodicity of 2D colorings of $\mathbb{Z}^2$ whose number of distinct shape-$D$ patterns is at most $|D|$.

## Key claim
A 2D configuration $c \in A^{\mathbb{Z}^2}$ satisfying the low-complexity condition $|L_D(c)| \le |D|$ has its annihilator ideal $\mathrm{Ann}(c) \subseteq \mathbb{C}[x^{\pm 1}]$ nontrivial, and any such $c$ is annihilated by a product of difference binomials $(x^{t_1}-1)\cdots(x^{t_m}-1)$ with pairwise linearly independent $t_i \in \mathbb{Z}^d$ — a "generalized periodicity" that reduces Nivat-type conjectures to ideal-structure analysis.

## Method
Encode a configuration as a formal Laurent power series $c(x) = \sum_u c_u x^u$ and study the annihilator ideal $\mathrm{Ann}(c) = \{f : fc = 0\}$ and periodizer ideal $\mathrm{Per}(c) = \{f : fc \text{ is two-periodic}\}$. Low complexity ($|L_D(c)| \le |D|$) gives a non-zero annihilator by elementary linear algebra (Lemma 1). In 2D, $\mathrm{Per}(c) = \langle f_1 \cdots f_m\rangle$ is principal, generated by a product of *line polynomials* (univariate in a monomial $x^u$); annihilation by a line polynomial in direction $u$ forces $nu$-periodicity. The order $\mathrm{Ord}(c) = m$ stratifies configurations; modular reduction extends the method to algebraic subshifts $S_f$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$.

## Result
(Thm 3) Any non-periodic 2D configuration violates $|L_D(c)| \le |D|$ for all but finitely many rectangles $D$ — asymptotic Nivat. (Thm 4, Szabados) Nivat holds for order-2 configurations (sums of two periodic configurations). (Thm 5) Uniformly recurrent low-complexity configurations on any *discrete convex* shape $D$ are periodic — generalizes beyond rectangles. (Thm 6, 7) Low-complexity SFTs on convex $D$ contain a periodic configuration, giving a decision algorithm for emptiness. (Thm 10) In algebraic subshift $S_f$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$: if $f$ has no line-polynomial factors then $c$ is two-periodic; if all line factors share a direction then $c$ is periodic (no shape assumption on $D$).

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. The annihilator-ideal / line-polynomial machinery is a non-obvious template for "low local complexity ⇒ global structure" arguments that could conceivably illuminate discrete combinatorics or extremal-graph problems in the arena, but no current Einstein problem reduces to 2D pattern complexity.

## Open questions / connections
- Full Nivat's conjecture (rectangles) and Cassaigne's generalization to all discrete convex shapes remain open; order-3 case is conjecturally the "true" hard case (sums need not be finitary).
- Do non-convex low-complexity shapes also force periodic elements in SFTs? Would extend the emptiness-decidability algorithm.
- Does an aperiodic low-complexity SFT exist in any dimension $d$? Unknown; all known low-complexity configurations decompose into periodic sublattices.
- Three-dimensional analog of $\mathrm{Ann}(c) = f_1 \cdots f_m H$ ideal structure (Thm 9) is unknown.
- Periodic cluster tiling problem (does every cluster tile with a co-tiler have a periodic co-tiler?) — solved in 2D [Bhattacharya 2016]; open in higher dimensions except $|D|$ prime [Szegedy 1998].
