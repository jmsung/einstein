# Equilibrium stresses and rigidity for infinite tensegrities and frameworks

**Authors:** Stephen Power  ·  **Year:** 2022  ·  **Source:** http://arxiv.org/abs/2207.14369v3

## One-line
Extends the Roth–Whiteley equilibrium-stress characterisation of first-order rigidity, and the prestress-stability ⇒ continuous-rigidity implication, from finite to countably infinite tensegrities and bar-joint frameworks via Banach-sequence-space methods.

## Key claim
For a countable cable-strut tensegrity $G(p)$ with uniform structure (bounded vertex degree and bounded member lengths) and $X \in \{c_0, \ell^q : 1 \le q < \infty\}$: $G(p)$ is first-order $X$-rigid iff the underlying bar-joint framework $\overline{G(p)}$ is first-order $X$-rigid AND there exists a proper $X'$-asymptotic equilibrium stress (Theorem 2.7). Separately, bounded prestress stability (BPS) with a summable equilibrium stress implies directed continuous rigidity (DCR) for infinite frameworks with bounded bar lengths (Theorem 3.17), yielding the hierarchy IR ⇒ BIR ⇒ BPS ⇒ WBPS ⇒ DCR.

## Method
Replace the finite-dimensional dual-cone / Farkas dichotomy by Hahn–Banach separation for closed convex cones in Banach sequence spaces, plus a relative second-dual-cone identity $\hat{C} = C^{**} \cap \hat{Y}$ (Lemma 2.10). For the prestress-stability ⇒ continuous-rigidity direction, the paper gives a new short proof using only Connelly's stress-energy function $E_\omega(q) = \sum_{i<j} \omega_{ij}|q_i - q_j|^2$: $E_\omega$ is strictly increasing along the cone of velocity perturbations near any nontrivial flex, contradicting bar-length preservation along any continuous motion. The argument transfers to the infinite setting once $\omega$ is summable and flexes are bounded.

## Result
(1) A proper $X'$-asymptotic equilibrium stress is constructed (Lemma 2.11) from the failure of the alternative "there exists $u \in X \otimes \mathbb{R}^d$ with $R(G(p))u$ a nonzero element of $\mathbb{R}^E_+$", via separation of the closed cone $C(X) \subset X' \otimes \mathbb{R}^d$ generated by the rows of the rigidity matrix. (2) Counterexamples (Examples 2.1, 2.2) show the literal Roth–Whiteley equivalence fails for infinite tensegrities — e.g. the triangular-cable lattice admits the contractive flex $(x,y) \mapsto (x/2, y/2)/2$ — but the $c_0$-rigid version holds. (3) Infinite frameworks exist that are infinitesimally rigid yet continuously flexible (§3.5, Figure 6); BPS rules this out in the directed-motion sense.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. The closed-convex-cone / Hahn–Banach separation machinery for asymptotic equilibrium stresses is a clean analytic counterpart to Farkas-style dichotomies that occasionally surface in LP-bound and SDP-relaxation arguments for packing/extremal problems, and the stress-energy second-derivative trick is a generic "prestress certifies local rigidity" pattern that could inform basin-rigidity diagnostics on configuration spaces.

## Open questions / connections
- Does the kagome periodic framework in $\mathbb{R}^2$ admit a continuous (or smooth) motion with uniformly bounded joint deviations? (Stated open in §2.)
- Can directed continuous rigidity (DCR) be relaxed to a stronger continuous-rigidity notion for countable frameworks without admitting the "exotic Kempe-style" counterexamples of Owen–Power [16,18]?
- Extends Roth–Whiteley [20], Connelly [4,5], Connelly–Whiteley [8], Asimow–Roth [2,3]; complements Ashton's continuous-tensegrity thesis [1] and Kitson–Power infinite-graph rigidity [13,14].
