# Total positivity, Schubert positivity, and Geometric Satake

**Authors:** T. Lam, K. Rietsch  ·  **Year:** 2012  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1203.1682

## One-line
Proves three notions of positivity on the centralizer $X = (G^\vee)^F$ of a principal nilpotent coincide, and parametrizes the totally nonnegative part of $X$ as $\mathbb{R}^n_{\geq 0}$ for arbitrary type.

## Key claim
The affine Schubert positive part $X^{>0}_{\mathrm{af}}$, Lusztig's totally positive part $X^{>0}$, and the Mirkovic-Vilonen positive part $X^{>0}_{\mathrm{MV}}$ all coincide (Theorem 7.1); and $X^{>0} \cong \mathbb{R}^n_{>0}$, $X^{\geq 0} \cong \mathbb{R}^n_{\geq 0}$ (Theorem 7.3), confirming Rietsch's conjecture in arbitrary type.

## Method
Combines Ginzburg/Peterson's isomorphism $H_*(\mathrm{Gr}_G) \cong \mathcal{O}(X)$, the geometric Satake correspondence (matrix coefficients of MV-cycles realize fundamental representations of $G^\vee$), Kumar–Nori positivity of effective classes in $H_*(\mathrm{Gr}_G)$, and a Berenstein–Zelevinsky criterion for total positivity via chamber minors. Parametrization uses Perron–Frobenius applied to the indecomposable nonnegative matrix of quantum-Schubert multiplication operators in $qH^*(G/P)$.

## Result
Establishes $X^{>0}_{\mathrm{af}} \subseteq X^{>0}_{\mathrm{MV}} \subseteq X^{>0} \subseteq X^{>0}_{\mathrm{af}}$ as a chain of equalities; the parametrization map $\pi^P_{>0} = (q_1^P, \ldots, q_k^P): X^{P,>0} \to \mathbb{R}^k_{>0}$ is a homeomorphism (bijective + continuous + closed). In type $A_n$, $X$ is the group of lower-triangular unipotent Toeplitz matrices, so this is a finite-dimensional analog of the Edrei–Thoma theorem.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. Touches positivity structures (canonical bases, cluster algebras, total positivity) that occasionally surface in LP-bound / SDP positivity arguments for sphere packing or autocorrelation, but the paper itself is representation-theoretic rather than extremal.

## Open questions / connections
- Whether the affine Schubert basis $\{\xi^w\}$ is literally a "dual canonical basis" for $\mathcal{O}(X)$ (suggested but not proved).
- Extends Rietsch's type-$A$ Toeplitz parametrization (J. Amer. Math. Soc. 16, 2003); the mirror-symmetric construction of Rietsch (Nagoya 2006) gives a different proof of part of this for type $A$ but misses the positivity-of-bases content.
- Appendix A verifies quantum-Schubert = affine-Schubert positivity in type $C_n$; equivalence in general type remains conjectural (Conjecture 7.2).
