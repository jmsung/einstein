# Lefschetz Theory for Exterior Algebras and Fermionic Diagonal Coinvariants

**Authors:** Jongwon Kim, B. Rhoades  ·  **Year:** 2020  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.10031

## One-line
Determines the bigraded $W$-module structure of the fermionic diagonal coinvariant ring $DR_W := \wedge(V \oplus V^*) / \langle \wedge(V \oplus V^*)^W_+ \rangle$ for any irreducible complex reflection group $W$, using a novel "bigraded Lefschetz" property of the exterior algebra.

## Key claim
For $W$ irreducible of rank $n$: $\dim DR_W = \binom{2n}{n+1}$, the ideal is principal generated by the Casimir $\delta_W = \sum \theta_i \xi_i$, and $[(DR_W)_{i,j}] = [\wedge^i V]\cdot[\wedge^j V^*] - [\wedge^{i-1}V]\cdot[\wedge^{j-1}V^*]$ in the Grothendieck ring (with $i+j \le n$); boundary pieces give Narayana numbers $\dim(DR_W)_{k,n-k} = \text{Nar}(n+1, k+1)$ summing to $\text{Cat}(n+1)$.

## Method
Type-uniform algebraic combinatorics. Core tool: prove that multiplication by $\delta_n^r$ on the bigraded exterior algebra $\wedge\{\Theta_n, \Xi_n\}$ is a bijection $\wedge_{i,j} \to \wedge_{n-j, n-i}$ when $r = n-i-j$ — a "bigraded Hard Lefschetz" with a $W$-invariant Lefschetz element (unusual: linear forms in exterior algebras square to zero, and Lefschetz elements in coinvariants are normally non-invariant). The bijection reduces via a carefully signed basis $v(A,B)$ to the Hara–Watanabe theorem that the Boolean-lattice incidence matrix between complementary ranks is invertible. Standard monomial basis is then given by non-negative decorated Motzkin-like paths under an explicit term order.

## Result
Full bigraded Frobenius image for $W = S_n$ in terms of Kronecker products of hook Schur functions $s_{(n-i, 1^i)} * s_{(n-j, 1^j)}$; explicit lattice-path basis $\Pi(n)_{\ge 0}$ with weight $\prod \text{wt}(s_k)$ giving $\text{Hilb}(DR_W; q, t) = \sum_\sigma q^{\deg_\theta(\sigma)} t^{\deg_\xi(\sigma)}$; for the $n$-dim permutation rep, $\dim DR_n = \binom{2n-1}{n}$ (resolving a Zabrocki conjecture) and graded sign-multiplicity equals $[n]_{q,t}$.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. Closest hooks are conceptual: the Lefschetz/Poincaré-duality machinery for graded algebras (cf. Adiprasito–Huh–Katz Chow rings of matroids) and Catalan/Narayana combinatorics, which can appear in extremal-graph and combinatorial problems but is not central to current Einstein problems.

## Open questions / connections
- Geometric proof of the bigraded Hard Lefschetz Theorem 3.2 for $\wedge(V \oplus V^*)$ (Question 7.1) — possibly via holomorphic tangent space at the origin of $\mathbb{C}^n \oplus \mathbb{C}^n$.
- Extend to mixed commuting + anticommuting superspace $\mathbb{C}[X_n, Y_n] \otimes \wedge\{\Theta_n, \Xi_n\}$ and connect to the Delta Conjecture (Haglund–Remmel–Wilson) and Carlsson–Oblomkov / Haglund–Sergel parking-function bases (Problem 7.2).
- Multigraded structure of $R(k \times n) = \wedge\{\Theta_{k \times n}\} / \langle \wedge\{\Theta_{k \times n}\}^{S_n}_+ \rangle$ for $k > 2$ (Question 7.3); Lefschetz approach blocked because $\delta_S^2 = 0$ for odd $|S|$.
