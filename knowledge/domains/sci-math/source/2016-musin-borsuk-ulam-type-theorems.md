# Borsuk–Ulam type theorems for G-spaces with applications to Tucker type lemmas

**Authors:** O. Musin, A. Volovikov  ·  **Year:** 2016  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1612.07314

## One-line
Extends the Borsuk–Ulam theorem and Tucker's combinatorial lemma from $\mathbb{Z}_2$-actions to arbitrary finite group $G$-actions on bounded spaces, manifolds, and simplicial complexes, using cohomological and topological $G$-indices.

## Key claim
For a free $G$-simplicial complex $X$, $\text{t-ind}_G X \geq n$ if and only if every equivariant $(G,n)$-labeling of every equivariant triangulation of $X$ has a complementary edge (Theorem 7.1) — a clean iff-characterization of the topological $G$-index via discrete labelings. Companion Theorem 5.4: any continuous $f: M^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$ on a compact orientable manifold with $G$ acting freely on $\partial M$ and $\text{ind}_G \partial M = n-1$ has $f^{-1}(0) \neq \emptyset$.

## Method
Cohomological index $\text{ind}_G X$ (Schwarz's homological genus minus 1) and topological index $\text{t-ind}_G X$ (minimal $n$ admitting equivariant map to $J_{n+1}(G) = G * \cdots * G$). Core lever is property 8 of the index: if $f: X \to Y$ is equivariant and indices agree, then $f^*$ on top-dim cohomology is nontrivial. Krasnosel'skii's degree-modulo-$|G|$ theorem and Smith sequences supply the algebraic backbone; "camomile" construction $C = GX$ lifts non-equivariant $X$ to equivariant setting for Bourgin–Yang dimension bounds on $f^{-1}(Y_0)$.

## Result
Establishes: (i) Theorem 7.1 — $G$-Tucker lemma via topological index; (ii) Theorem 7.3 — for compact orientable PL-manifold $M^n$ with $\partial M \cong S^{n-1}$ admitting free simplicial $G$-action, every $(G,n)$-labeling equivariant on $\partial T$ has a complementary edge; (iii) Theorem 6.3 (Bourgin–Yang) — $\dim f^{-1}(Y_0) \geq n - \text{ind}_G(Y \setminus Y_0)$; (iv) resolves Baum–Dąbrowski–Hajac Conjecture 2.2 for compact/finite-dim paracompact free $G$-spaces: no equivariant map $X * G \to X$.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. None of the 23 Einstein Arena problems currently in scope (sphere packing, autocorrelation, kissing numbers, Sidon-like extremal combinatorics) reduce to $G$-equivariant zero-set / labeling arguments — this is an equivariant algebraic topology paper aimed at fixed-point and combinatorial-lemma theory.

## Open questions / connections
- Baum–Dąbrowski–Hajac Conjecture 2.2 remains open for general compact Hausdorff (non-finite-dim) group actions on $C^*$-algebras.
- Extends Conner–Floyd, Yang, Krasnosel'skii, Dold, Schwarz lineage of equivariant-index theory; relates Lusternik–Schnirelmann category / Schwarz genus / Smale's topological complexity (same notion, three names).
- Could inform combinatorial-existence proofs (Sperner/Tucker/KKM analogs on manifolds) if a future arena problem ever requires a topological-existence certificate rather than an explicit construction.
