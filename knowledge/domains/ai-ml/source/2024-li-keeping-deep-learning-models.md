# Keeping Deep Learning Models in Check: A History-Based Approach to Mitigate Overfitting

**Authors:** Hao Li, Gopi Krishnan Rajbahadur, Dayi Lin, C. Bezemer, Zhen Ming Jiang  ·  **Year:** 2024  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.10359

## One-line
Trains a time-series classifier on validation-loss curves to detect overfit DL models and stop training earlier than early-stopping heuristics.

## Key claim
OverfitGuard (KNN-DTW on validation-loss curves) detects overfitting at F1 = 0.91 (≥5% over correlation-based baselines) and stops training ≥32% earlier than standard early stopping while matching or exceeding its rate of returning the optimal-epoch model.

## Method
Treat the per-epoch validation-loss sequence as a time series and train six classifiers (KNN-DTW, HMM-GMM, TSF, TSBF, SAX-VSM, BOSSVS) on a simulated corpus of 419 labeled training histories generated by training 12 NN architectures on 36 Proben1 dataset splits for 1,000 epochs each. At inference, validation losses are linearly interpolated to a fixed length (KNN-DTW handles variable length); for prevention, the classifier is applied either to the full observed history or a rolling window (e.g., 40–80 epochs) and training is halted upon a positive overfit prediction, returning the epoch with the lowest validation loss so far.

## Result
On a real-world evaluation set of 40 published training histories (29 overfit), KNN-DTW gives F1 ≈ 0.91 vs ≤0.86 for Spearman/Pearson/time-lagged Pearson correlation thresholds. For prevention with a rolling window of 40 epochs, KNN-DTW achieves the same average accuracy (0.42) as early stopping at patience 40 but with median delay 27 vs 40 epochs (Mann–Whitney significant, medium–large Cliff's delta); whole-history KNN-DTW reaches 95% optimal rate at median delay 43.5 epochs vs ~85% for early stopping at comparable patience.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie — this is a software-engineering / DL-training paper, not a math-optimization one. The only tangential relevance is methodological: the closed-loop "monitor validation curve, stop when curve diagnostic fires" pattern echoes the project's own equioscillation/active-pair convergence diagnostics for optimizer halting, but the techniques and problem domain are disjoint from sphere packing, autocorrelation, kissing numbers, etc.

## Open questions / connections
- Would a curve-shape classifier on optimizer loss histories (e.g., basin-hopping, CMA-ES) detect "stuck in suboptimal basin" earlier than patience-based heuristics? Likely off-target for this project but possibly useful for compute budgeting.
- KNN-DTW inference cost is non-trivial (O(n²) DTW); paper flags real-time optimization as future work.
- Real-world test set is only 40 samples — generalization claims are weak; relevance to non-DL optimization curves is unestablished.
