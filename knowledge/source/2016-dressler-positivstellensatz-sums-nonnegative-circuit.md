# A Positivstellensatz for Sums of Nonnegative Circuit Polynomials

**Authors:** Mareike Dressler, Sadik Iliman, T. Wolff  ·  **Year:** 2016  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.06010

## One-line
Establishes a Positivstellensatz for SONC (sums of nonnegative circuit polynomials) certificates, giving a converging hierarchy of lower bounds for constrained polynomial optimization solvable via relative entropy programming instead of SDP.

## Key claim
For any $f \in \mathbb{R}[x]$ strictly positive on a compact basic semi-algebraic set $K = \{g_i \geq 0\}$, there exist $d, q \in \mathbb{N}^*$, SONC polynomials $s_j \in C_{n,2d}$, and products $H_j \in R_q(K)$ of the constraint polynomials such that $f(x) = \sum_{j=1}^{\rho_q} s_j(x) H_j(x)$ (Theorem 4.8). The resulting bounds $f_{\text{sonc}}^{(d,q)} \uparrow f_K^*$ as $d, q \to \infty$.

## Method
Circuit polynomials $f = \sum_{j=0}^r f_{\alpha(j)} x^{\alpha(j)} + f_\beta x^\beta$ (simplex Newton polytope, single interior term) are nonnegative iff $|f_\beta| \leq \Theta_f = \prod_j (f_{\alpha(j)}/\lambda_j)^{\lambda_j}$ where $\lambda_j$ are barycentric coordinates of $\beta$. The authors first prove the SONC cone $C_{n,2d}$ is full-dimensional in $P_{n,2d}$ (Theorem 4.3), then apply Marshall's Archimedean preprime/module representation theorem (Theorem 4.7) using a $P$-module $M = \sum s(x) H(x)$ over the preprime generated by constraints (plus redundant cube inequalities $N \pm x_i \geq 0$). Computation reduces to a relative entropy program (REP) leveraging the joint convexity of $D(\nu, \zeta) = \sum \nu_j \log(\nu_j/\zeta_j)$.

## Result
Theorem 4.3: $C_{n,2d}$ is full-dimensional in $P_{n,2d}$ for all $n, d$. Theorem 4.8: explicit SONC Positivstellensatz with $\rho_q \leq \binom{s+2n+q}{q}$ terms (exponential in $q$). Theorem 5.2: the hierarchy $f_{\text{sonc}}^{(d,q)}$ converges to $f_K^*$. Theorem 5.3: the optimization program (5.3) is a REP, hence efficiently solvable by interior point methods. Lemma 4.1: SONC is not closed under multiplication (so not a preordering/quadratic module), unlike SOS.

## Why it matters here
General background for optimization theory — relevant if any Einstein Arena problem involves polynomial optimization over semi-algebraic sets where SDP/Lasserre relaxation is too large (sparse high-degree polynomials). Offers an SDP-free alternative when SOS hierarchies blow up; ties to the wiki's existing coverage of LP/SDP relaxation methods.

## Open questions / connections
- Does a Putinar-equivalent representation with $q = 1$ always exist (linear rather than exponential number of terms)?
- Finite convergence conditions analogous to Lasserre's are open.
- Primal/dual relationship between SAGE cone (Chandrasekaran–Shah) and SONC cone.
- Practical strategies to prune redundant circuit polynomials in (5.3); combining SOS and SONC certificates.
