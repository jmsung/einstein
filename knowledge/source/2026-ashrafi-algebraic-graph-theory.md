# Algebraic Graph Theory

**Authors:** A. Ashrafi, M. J. Nadjafi-Arani  ·  **Year:** 2026  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20890

## One-line
Lecture-note style monograph on algebraic graph theory, covering automorphism groups, vertex/arc/distance-transitive graphs, Moore graphs, incidence structures, Steiner systems / Mathieu groups, and cores of graphs.

## Key claim
A unified expository treatment showing how symmetry (automorphism groups, transitive actions, Cayley/coset constructions) and combinatorial design (generalized polygons, Steiner systems $S(5,6,12)$, $S(5,8,24)$) classify and constrain highly symmetric finite graphs, with worked classifications such as $\mathrm{Aut}(P)\cong S_5$ for the Petersen graph and $M_{11},M_{12},M_{22}\cong\mathrm{Aut}$ of the corresponding Steiner systems.

## Method
Standard algebraic-graph-theory toolkit: group actions on vertex/edge/arc sets, orbit-counting and stabilizer arguments, eigenvalue analysis of strongly regular / distance-regular graphs (Moore-graph parameter equation), Levi/incidence-graph constructions, coset graphs, and induction on shortest odd cycles for core theorems. Sharp $t$-transitivity arguments drive the Mathieu/Steiner uniqueness proofs.

## Result
Reproves the classical canon: $\mathrm{Aut}(C_n)\cong D_{2n}$; $\mathrm{Aut}(P)\cong S_5$ via maximum independent sets (stars $S_i$); $\mathrm{Aut}(\text{Tutte–Coxeter})\cong S_6\rtimes C_2$ of order 1440; Moore-graph existence restricted to $\{C_5, K_3, \text{Petersen}, \text{Hoffman–Singleton}, ?n{=}3250\}$; $K(n,k)$ is a core for $n\ge 2k+1$ (via Erdős–Ko–Rado); cubic vertex-transitive cores must be $K_2$, an odd cycle, or the graph itself; $S_6$'s outer automorphism arises from the $M_{12}$ embedding.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. Closest hooks: the Steiner system $S(5,8,24)$ underlies the Leech lattice (sphere-packing in $\mathbb{R}^{24}$), and strongly-regular / Moore-graph eigenvalue bounds parallel LP-bound techniques used in kissing-number and code/packing problems.

## Open questions / connections
- Classify all $(v,k,i)$ for which $\mathrm{Aut}(J(v,k,i))\cong \mathrm{Sym}(v)$ (stated open in the text).
- Existence of a Moore graph of valency 57 on 3250 vertices — long-standing open problem touched in Ch. 5.
- Connections to Cohn–Elkies / Viazovska sphere-packing program via Leech-lattice construction from $S(5,8,24)$ and to LP/SDP bounds via strongly regular graph spectra.
