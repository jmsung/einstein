# Enumeration of points, lines, planes, etc

**Authors:** June Huh, Botong Wang  ·  **Year:** 2016  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.05484

## One-line
Proves the Dowling–Wilson "top-heavy" conjecture for realizable geometric lattices: low-rank Whitney numbers are dominated by high-rank ones, with an explicit order-preserving injection.

## Key claim
For a finite point set $E$ spanning a projective space $P$ of dimension $r$, with $L$ the lattice of flats it generates, $|L^p| \le |L^{r-q}|$ for $p \le q$ with $p+q \le r$, witnessed by an injection $\iota : L^p \to L^{r-q}$ with $x \le \iota(x)$. Equivalently, the multiplication map $L^{r-2p} : B^p(M) \to B^{r-p}(M)$ on the graded Möbius algebra is injective whenever the matroid $M$ is realizable over some field.

## Method
Introduce the graded Möbius algebra $B^*(M)$ generated by symbols $y_F$ for flats $F$, embed it via $y_i \mapsto \beta_i$ into the Chow ring $A^*(M)$ of the matroid (Feichtner–Yuzvinsky), and identify $B^*(M) \otimes \mathbb{Q}_\ell$ with the étale cohomology $H^{2*}(Y_{\mathcal{A}}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell)$ of the Ardila–Boocher "reciprocal plane" variety. The injectivity then follows from the BBD decomposition theorem applied to $X_{\mathcal{A}} \to (\mathbb{P}^1)^n$ and hard Lefschetz on intersection cohomology, with $L = \sum y_i$ playing the role of an ample class.

## Result
Settles top-heavy (part 1) for all field-realizable geometric lattices, and shows the Whitney number differences $|L^0|, |L^1|-|L^0|, \dots, |L^{p+1}|-|L^p|$ form the $h$-vector of a shellable simplicial complex (an $M$-sequence). Conjectured to extend to all matroids (Conjecture 7). Remark 15 also derives a one-positive-eigenvalue property of the Hodge–Riemann form, implying $b(M) b_{ij}(M) \le 2\, b_i(M) b_j(M)$ for bases.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie. The Hodge-theoretic / decomposition-theorem framework for matroid invariants is a deep template for "log-concavity / unimodality via geometry," potentially relevant background for extremal combinatorics problems on the arena that touch matroid or flag inequalities.

## Open questions / connections
- Conjecture 7: does Theorem 6 hold for non-realizable matroids? (Resolved later by Braden–Huh–Matherne–Proudfoot–Wang.)
- Rota's unimodality conjecture for the *upper half* of $|L^p|$ remains open.
- Mason's negative-correlation conjecture for bases ($b_i b_j \ge b\, b_{ij}$ ratio bounded by 1) — counterexample at ratio 1.02 by Seymour–Welsh; how large can the ratio be? Connection to [[hodge-theory-matroids]] and [[ahk-log-concavity]].
