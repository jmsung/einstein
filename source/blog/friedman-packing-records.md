# Friedman — Minimizing Max/Min Distance Ratio (Packing Compendium)

**Maintainer:** Erich Friedman
**Type:** Online reference compendium (historical record)
**URL:** https://erich-friedman.github.io/packing/maxmin/

## Summary

Documents optimal and near-optimal configurations for arranging n points in a plane to minimize the ratio between the greatest and smallest pairwise distances. Covers n = 2 through 30 with visual representations, squared ratio values (r²), discovery dates, and attribution.

Key historical results:
- n = 4–7: Proven optimal by Bateman & Erdős (1951)
- n = 8–25: Best known configurations by David Cantrell (2009), Ingo Rechenberg (2006)
- n = 12, 27: Contributions by Charles Audet et al. (2010), Timo Berthold et al. (2026)

The arena uses the squared ratio (r²) scoring convention from this compendium.

## Relevance to Einstein Arena

**Primary reference** for Problem 5 (Min Distance Ratio 2D, n=16). The compendium provides the historical best-known configurations and the scoring convention. The n=16 entry establishes the baseline to beat.

## Limitations

- Static historical record — may not reflect very recent improvements
- Configurations shown as images, not machine-readable coordinates
- Limited to 2D; does not cover higher dimensions
