# Ribet — On Modular Representations of Gal(Q̄/Q) Arising from Modular Forms

**Author:** Kenneth A. Ribet
**Year:** 1990
**Journal:** Inventiones Mathematicae, Vol. 100, pp. 431–476
**DOI:** 10.1007/BF01231195

## Summary

Ribet proves Serre's epsilon conjecture: if ρ̄ is a mod-p Galois representation attached to a weight-2 newform of level Np (with p ∤ N), and ρ̄ is irreducible, then ρ̄ also arises from a newform of level N. This "level-lowering" result is the step that reduces FLT to the Taniyama-Shimura conjecture.

Applied to FLT: the Frey curve E: y² = x(x-a^p)(x+b^p) has conductor divisible by huge primes from abc. Ribet's theorem strips away all odd prime level, lowering to level 2. Since S₂(Γ₀(2)) = 0, the representation cannot exist — contradicting the assumption that E (and hence the Fermat counterexample) exists.

## Key Techniques

- **Level-lowering via multiplicity one**: Representations at higher level descend to lower level
- **Shimura's construction**: Using Jacobians of modular curves J₀(N)
- **Character group analysis**: Local behavior at primes of multiplicative reduction
- **Ihara's lemma**: Key ingredient for the multiplicity argument

## Relevance to Proof Problems

Second link in the FLT chain. Demonstrates that modular representation theory can eliminate impossible configurations — a proof strategy applicable to any problem where Galois representations arise.
