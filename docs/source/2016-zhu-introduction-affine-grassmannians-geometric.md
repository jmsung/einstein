# An introduction to affine Grassmannians and the geometric Satake equivalence

**Authors:** Xinwen Zhu  ·  **Year:** 2016  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1603.05593

## One-line
Expanded PCMI lecture notes giving a self-contained introduction to affine Grassmannians $\mathrm{Gr}_G$ as ind-projective schemes, their Beilinson–Drinfeld generalizations, and the geometric Satake equivalence linking perverse sheaves on $\mathrm{Gr}_G$ to representations of the Langlands dual group $\hat G$.

## Key claim
The Satake category $\mathrm{Sat}_G$ of $L^+G$-equivariant perverse sheaves on $\mathrm{Gr}_G$, equipped with the fusion product (built from Beilinson–Drinfeld Grassmannians over the Ran space), is a neutral Tannakian category whose Tannakian group is canonically isomorphic to the Langlands dual group $\hat G$ — giving an equivalence $\mathrm{Sat}_G \simeq \mathrm{Rep}(\hat G)$.

## Method
Construct $\mathrm{Gr}_G(R) = \{(\mathcal{E},\beta)\}$ classifying $G$-torsors on the disc $D_R$ with trivialization on $D_R^*$; represent it as an ind-projective scheme using Beauville–Laszlo gluing and embeddings into $\mathrm{Gr}_{GL_n}$. Globalize to Beilinson–Drinfeld Grassmannians $\mathrm{Gr}_{X^I}$ which exhibit a factorization property over disjoint configurations, yielding the fusion / convolution product. Apply Mirković–Vilonen theory on semi-infinite orbits and weight functors via hyperbolic localization to recover the dual root datum.

## Result
Constructs $\mathrm{Gr}_G$ for any smooth affine $k[[t]]$-group as an ind-scheme of ind-finite type, ind-projective when $G$ is reductive; classifies Schubert varieties indexed by dominant coweights $X_\bullet(T)^+$ with the Bruhat order; computes the Picard group of $\mathrm{Gr}_G$ and of $\mathrm{Bun}_G$ for simple simply-connected $G$; proves one-point and adèlic uniformization $\mathrm{Bun}_G \simeq G(F)\backslash G(\mathbb{A})/G(\mathcal{O})$; identifies global sections of line bundles on $\mathrm{Bun}_G$ with conformal blocks; and establishes the geometric Satake equivalence in forms suitable for the (geometric) Langlands program, recovering the classical Satake isomorphism as a shadow.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie — this is pure algebraic geometry / representation theory (Langlands program), not a source of optimization techniques, packing bounds, or combinatorial extremal results relevant to the 23 Einstein Arena problems.

## Open questions / connections
- Extensions to $p$-adic / mixed-characteristic affine Grassmannians (Bhatt–Scholze, Zhu) and twisted / ramified versions.
- Quantizations of transversal slices in $\mathrm{Gr}_G$ via Yangians (Kamnitzer–Webster–Weekes–Yacobi).
- Applications to Shimura-variety reduction mod $p$, affine Deligne–Lusztig varieties, and Kisin varieties (deformations of Galois representations).
