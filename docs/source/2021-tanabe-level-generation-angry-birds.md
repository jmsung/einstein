# Level generation for angry birds with sequential VAE and latent variable evolution

**Authors:** Takumi Tanabe, Kazuto Fukuchi, Jun Sakuma, Youhei Akimoto  ·  **Year:** 2021  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.06106

## One-line
Trains a sequential (LSTM) VAE on word-tokenized "drop-from-top" encodings of Angry Birds levels, then uses CMA-ES latent variable evolution to steer generated levels toward desired play features.

## Key claim
Encoding levels as a sequence of "drop" tokens (rather than a 2D image grid) and treating row patterns as words drastically improves the probability of generating gravity-stable, playable Angry Birds levels: 96/100 stable for the proposed word-based sequential VAE vs 0–10/100 for character-based or image-based (WGAN-GP) baselines, with bigram diversity reaching 149% of the training set.

## Method
Each level is encoded bottom-up as a sequence $S=(s_1,\dots,s_{30})$ where time step $t$ specifies which object types (out of $|O|=61$) drop at which of $94$ horizontal bins; vertical coordinates emerge from physics. Rows are mapped to one-hot "words" over a vocabulary $\mathcal{W}$ built from the training set, embedded via word2vec CBOW into $\mathbb{R}^{50}$, and fed to an LSTM $\beta$-VAE with $\dim z = 60$, trained with KL annealing and word drop. LVE then runs CMA-ES (pycma) over $z\in[-3,3]^{60}$ — sometimes reparameterized as $z\sim\mathcal{N}(\beta,\alpha I)$ — to minimize objectives like $-N_{\text{Pigs}}$ or AI-agent-play scores such as $\max(5-N_{\text{Birds}},N_{\text{RemBirds}})\cdot 10 + N_{\text{Blocks}}$.

## Result
On a tiny dataset of 180 training levels generated by IratusAves, the proposed model reproduces ~92% of training unigrams and exceeds training bigram count by 49%, while WGAN-GP image-based generators produce 0–6 stable levels per 100. LVE successfully drives $N_{\text{Pigs}}$ from baseline to 13 pigs by generation 100 and minimizes simulation-based difficulty/aesthetic objectives without latent-collapse, confirming the latent code carries controllable signal.

## Why it matters here
General background; no direct arena tie — this is a procedural-content-generation / game-AI paper, not a math-optimization paper. The transferable nugget is methodological: when a generative model must respect a hard physical constraint (gravity, non-overlap), changing the *encoding* (sequential drop-order rather than coordinates) can convert a near-zero feasibility rate into ~96%, an instance of the broader "parameterization-selection > optimizer-tuning" lesson relevant to packing/configuration problems.

## Open questions / connections
- Constraint-handling inside LVE itself — the authors note LVE-generated intermediates can become unstable even when the base generator is stable; how to penalize during CMA-ES search?
- Does the "build by sequential drop" trick generalize to other gravity-constrained or hard-constraint generative tasks (e.g., sphere/disk packing initializations)?
- Latent-collapse in sequential VAEs (Bowman et al. 2016) is mitigated here by KL annealing + word drop; the question of *how much* latent information is retained vs randomness of $G$ remains only empirically checked.
