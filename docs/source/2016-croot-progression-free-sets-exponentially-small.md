# Progression-free sets in Z_4^n are exponentially small

**Authors:** Ernie Croot, V. Lev, P. Pach  ·  **Year:** 2016  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1605.01506

## One-line
Proves that any 3-term-progression-free subset of $\mathbb{Z}_4^n$ has size at most $4^{\gamma n}$ with $\gamma \approx 0.926 < 1$, the first exponential-savings bound of its kind via the polynomial method.

## Key claim
**Theorem 1:** For $n \geq 1$, if $A \subseteq \mathbb{Z}_4^n$ is progression-free (no distinct $a,b,c$ with $a+b=2c$), then $|A| \leq 4^{\gamma n}$ where $\gamma := \max_{0<\varepsilon<0.25} \tfrac{1}{2}(H(0.5-\varepsilon) + H(2\varepsilon)) \approx 0.926$ and $H$ is the binary entropy function. Corollary: for any finite abelian $G$ with $n := \mathrm{rk}_4(G)$, $r_3(G) \leq 4^{-(1-\gamma)n}|G|$.

## Method
**Polynomial method** (no Fourier analysis / density increment). Core Lemma 1: if $P$ is a multilinear polynomial of total degree $\leq d$ in $n$ variables over a field $F$, $A \subseteq F^n$ has $|A| > 2\sum_{i \leq d/2}\binom{n}{i}$, and $P(a-b)=0$ for all distinct $a,b \in A$, then $P(0)=0$ — proved by splitting $P(x-y)$ into low/high-degree monomial halves and a linear-independence argument in $F^{2m}$. Apply over $F_2$ to the subgroup $F_n \cong \mathbb{Z}_2^n$ generated by involutions of $\mathbb{Z}_4^n$, with a coset-counting argument (Proposition 1) bounding how many $F_n$-cosets can be heavy in $A$, then integrate via the tensor power trick.

## Result
Improves Sanders [S11] ($r_3(\mathbb{Z}_4^n) = O(4^n / n(\log n)^\varepsilon)$, only polylog savings) to **exponential** savings: $r_3(\mathbb{Z}_4^n) \leq 4^{0.926 n}$, i.e., a factor of $4^{0.074 n}$ below the trivial bound. This is the first exponential reduction for any group of this Roth/Meshulam family — the precursor to the cap-set breakthrough $r_3(\mathbb{F}_3^n) = O(2.756^n)$ of Ellenberg–Gijswijt (2016), which adapted exactly this multilinear-polynomial / Croot–Lev–Pach lemma to $\mathbb{F}_3^n$.

## Why it matters here
General background for the **polynomial method** as a tool in extremal combinatorics — the same Croot–Lev–Pach lemma is the engine behind cap-set bounds and has been extended to sunflower-free sets, tri-colored sum-free sets, and other Einstein-Arena-adjacent extremal problems (sphere-packing kissing-style configurations, Sidon-set bounds). No direct arena tie to a specific problem, but underwrites the technique catalog for extremal-set / progression-free / forbidden-pattern questions.

## Open questions / connections
- Exact value of $r_3(\mathbb{Z}_4^n)$ — gap between $4^{0.926n}$ upper bound and the best construction (which gives roughly $3^n$ via lifting cap sets).
- Whether the polynomial method extends beyond prime-power groups of small exponent to general abelian groups with unbounded torsion.
- The remark relaxing multilinearity to bounded individual degree (via $f_\delta(n,d)$) suggests applicability to $\mathbb{Z}_m^n$ for larger $m$ — partially realized by Ellenberg–Gijswijt for $\mathbb{F}_q^n$.
- Tensor power trick at the end is generic — appears in many additive-combinatorics contexts as a tool for upgrading polynomial-prefactor bounds to clean exponential ones.
