# Phases of melonic quantum mechanics

**Authors:** F. Ferrari, F. I. S. Massolo  ┬Ę  **Year:** 2019  ┬Ę  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.06633

## One-line
Detailed study of two fermionic quantum-mechanical melonic models (U(n)┬▓ complex-SYK and a new U(n) model) as functions of mass $m$ and temperature $T$, mapping their phase diagrams, critical exponents, and chaos behavior.

## Key claim
The U(n)┬▓ model exhibits a line of first-order transitions in the $(m,T)$ plane terminating at a critical point $(m_c,T_c)$ with non-mean-field, asymmetric thermodynamic exponents and singular quasi-normal frequencies $\nu_{qn}$ and Lyapunov exponents $\eta_L$; the U(n) model has a $T=0$ quantum critical point at $m_*>0$, below which a gapless phase appears with spontaneously broken SL(2,$\mathbb{R}$) scale symmetry ŌĆö distinct exponents $\Delta_+ \ne \Delta_-$ govern $G(\tau)$ as $\tau \to \pm\infty$.

## Method
Two equivalent formulations at leading large-$N$ order: (i) fermionic matrix quantum mechanics in the new large-$D$ planar limit with tetrahedric ($\sqrt{d}$-enhanced) and pillow interactions, (ii) disordered SYK-like Hamiltonians with random couplings $\lambda_{ijkl}$, $\xi^i_{jkl}$. SchwingerŌĆōDyson equations are resummed over melonic diagrams; thermodynamics derived from a bilocal effective action; real-time correlators via SchwingerŌĆōKeldysh + KadanoffŌĆōBaym solved using Carlson's theorem. Numerical solution at cutoff $k_{UV}=2^{16}$ extracts Euclidean two/four-point functions, quasi-normal frequencies, and Lyapunov exponents (via both direct kernel inversion and Kitaev's largest-eigenvalue ansatz).

## Result
Confirmed line of 1st-order transitions for the U(n)┬▓ model with q-dependent critical exponents (varying with interaction degree $q\ge 4$); multi-q models show continuously varying critical points interpolating $q=4 \leftrightarrow q=6$. Chaos bound $\eta_L \le 2\pi T$ is saturated as $T \to 0$ in the SYK-like black-hole phase but **not** saturated in the U(n) gapless phase. The U(n) model's $T=0$ entropy $\sigma > 0$ for $0 \le m < m_*$, vanishing at $m_*$; heat-capacity coefficient $c$ jumps discontinuously to zero at $m_*$. The new IR ansatz $G(\tau) \sim b_\pm/|\tau|^{2\Delta_\pm}$ with $\Delta_- > \Delta_+$ established numerically with high confidence.
