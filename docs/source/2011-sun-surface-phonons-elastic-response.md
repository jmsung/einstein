# Surface phonons, elastic response, and conformal invariance in twisted kagome lattices

**Authors:** K. Sun, A. Souslov, Xiaoming Mao, T. Lubensky  ·  **Year:** 2011  ·  **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1112.1109

## One-line
Shows that 2D isostatic lattices built by rigidly rotating kagome triangles have vanishing bulk modulus, maximally negative Poisson ratio $\sigma = -1$, and a long-wavelength elastic theory that is a conformally invariant field theory whose zero modes are analytic (holographic) Rayleigh surface waves.

## Key claim
For the twisted kagome lattice with twist angle $\alpha$: $B = 0$ exactly, $\mu = \sqrt{3}k/8$, $\sigma = -1$; under free boundary conditions there are $2(N_x+N_y)-1$ zero modes (all surface modes for the simple $C_{3v}$ twist), while under PBCs only $2$ states of self-stress survive. The continuum theory is invariant under conformal transformations of the metric.

## Method
Maxwell–Calladine mode counting via equilibrium $H$ and compatibility $C = H^T$ matrices, with the rank-nullity identity $N_0 = dN - N_B + S$ relating zero modes to states of self-stress $S$. Long-wavelength effective field theory in displacement $\mathbf{u}(x)$ and intra-cell scalar $\phi(x)$, with gauge-like coupling $(\phi + \xi u_{ii})^2$ forcing $B=0$. Surface modes solved by analytic continuation $q_y = q_y' + i q_y''$ via transfer matrix; conformal zero modes identified as analytic functions $w(z) = u_x + i u_y$ of $z = x+iy$.

## Result
Bulk modulus vanishes for all $\alpha \neq 0$ while shear modulus is $\alpha$-independent, giving auxetic $\sigma = -1$. Under free BCs all floppy modes of the simple twisted lattice are surface Rayleigh waves with penetration depth $1/q_x$; characteristic frequency scale $\omega_\alpha \sim \sqrt{k}|\sin\alpha|$ and length $l_\alpha \sim 1/|\sin\alpha|$. A scaling law $q_y' = l_\alpha^{-1} f(q_x l_\alpha)$ holds with $f(\eta) \to \eta$ for $\eta \ll 1$ and $f(\eta) \to 4/3$ for $\eta \gg 1$. A $pgg$ variant mixes bulk and surface floppy modes.

## Open questions / connections
- 3D analogs via rotated tetrahedra in pyrochlore / zeolite / cristobalite lattices — anisotropic bulk-modulus vanishing planes.
- Surfaces along non-symmetry directions (e.g. $x=0$ cut) deferred to follow-up [Sun, Mao, Lubensky, unpublished].
- Analogy to topological edge states in electronic systems (Hasan–Kane, Qi–Zhang) — isostatic edge-mode count as a possible topological invariant.
