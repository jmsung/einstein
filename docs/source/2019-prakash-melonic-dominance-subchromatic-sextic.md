## One-line
Classifies the maximally-single-trace (MST) sextic interaction vertices for $O(N)^r$ tensor models with $r < q-1$ and proves the resulting large-$N$ free energy is dominated by (generalized) melonic diagrams.

## Key claim
For $q=6$ sextic MST interactions there are exactly three "subchromatic" vertices â€” the $r{=}3$ **prism**, the $r{=}3$ **wheel** ($K_{3,3}$), and the $r{=}4$ **octahedron** â€” and each defines a solvable large-$N$ theory whose surviving Feynman diagrams are generated by three melonic moves: propagatorâ†’elementary melon, propagatorâ†’elementary snail, and a new **vertex-expansion** move (needed only for the prism).

## Method
Enumerative combinatorial classification of MST interaction graphs by recursively adding colours to lower-rank cyclic skeletons, plus a 't Hooft scaling argument: for an order-$q$ MST vertex with $r$ colours, planarity of every two-colour fat-graph subgraph yields the bound $f_{\text{tot}} \le \frac{r}{4}(q-2)v + r$, fixing the 't Hooft coupling $\lambda = g N^{r(q-2)/4}$. Maximal (leading-$N$) diagrams saturate this bound; their structure is then analyzed via 1-cycles and 2-cycles (closed walks through one or two vertices) in the single-line Feynman diagram, with inequivalent 2-cycles classified using colour-permutation + automorphism orbit reduction.

## Result
The octahedron ($r{=}4$) and wheel ($K_{3,3}$, $r{=}3$) theories are dominated by conventionally melonic diagrams (the wheel adds only an elementary tadpole snail). The prism theory requires an extra "vertex-expansion" move â€” equivalent to the auxiliary-field rewriting of [Giombi-Klebanov-Popov-Prakash-Tarnopolsky 2018] â€” and the combined wheel+prism theory needs no new elementary melons beyond those two. Schwinger-Dyson gap equation reduces to $G^{-1}(x) = -\tilde\lambda^2 G(x)^6$ with kernel $K = (q-1)\tilde\lambda^2 G(x_{13})G(x_{24})G(x_{34})^4$, identical to the $q=6$ bosonic melonic theory of [Giombi-Klebanov-Tarnopolsky].
