# Serre — Sur les représentations modulaires de degré 2 de Gal(Q̄/Q)

**Author:** Jean-Pierre Serre
**Year:** 1987
**Journal:** Duke Mathematical Journal, Vol. 54, No. 1, pp. 179–230
**DOI:** 10.1215/S0012-7094-87-05413-5

## Summary

Serre formulates the conjecture that every odd, irreducible 2-dimensional mod-p Galois representation ρ: Gal(Q̄/Q) → GL₂(F_p) arises from a modular form — specifically from a mod-p eigenform of prescribed weight and level. The paper gives precise recipes for the minimal weight k(ρ) and conductor N(ρ) in terms of local properties of ρ.

The "epsilon conjecture" (now Ribet's theorem) — that the Frey curve's mod-p representation cannot be modular — is identified here as the key link between Taniyama-Shimura and Fermat's Last Theorem.

## Key Concepts

- **Serre's modularity conjecture**: odd irreducible ρ̄: G_Q → GL₂(F_p) is modular
- **Weight and level recipes**: k(ρ) and N(ρ) predicted from local Galois data
- **Epsilon conjecture**: level-lowering prediction that connects FLT to modularity
- **Ramification analysis**: local-global principle for mod-p representations

## Relevance to Proof Problems

First link in the FLT proof chain. Serre's conjecture was fully proved by Khare-Wintenberger (2009). Any arena proof problem involving Galois representations or modularity would build on this framework.
