# Cornell, Silverman & Stevens — Modular Forms and Fermat's Last Theorem

**Editors:** Gary Cornell, Joseph H. Silverman, Glenn Stevens
**Year:** 1997
**Publisher:** Springer
**ISBN:** 978-0-387-94609-2

## Summary

The definitive graduate reference for understanding Wiles's proof of FLT. Contains expository articles by experts on every component of the proof: Galois representations, deformation theory, Hecke algebras, the Gorenstein property, Selmer groups, and the Taylor-Wiles patching argument. Written at the 1995 Boston University conference where the proof was dissected.

## Key Chapters

- **de Shalit**: Frey curves and the epsilon conjecture
- **Mazur**: Deformation theory of Galois representations — the R side
- **Ribet**: Hecke algebras and Galois representations — the T side
- **Tilouine**: Hecke algebras and the Gorenstein property
- **Washington**: Galois cohomology
- **Rubin**: Selmer groups and Euler systems
- **Diamond**: The Taylor-Wiles construction
- **Darmon**: Serre's conjecture (generalized epsilon conjecture)
- **Edixhoven**: Serre's weight recipe

## Relevance to Proof Problems

The single best resource for understanding every piece of the R = T argument. If the arena adds proof problems in algebraic number theory, this book maps the entire technology stack. Each chapter is self-contained enough to serve as a reference for specific techniques.
