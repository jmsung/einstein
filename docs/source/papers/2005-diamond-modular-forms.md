# Diamond & Shurman — A First Course in Modular Forms

**Authors:** Fred Diamond, Jerry Shurman
**Year:** 2005
**Publisher:** Springer (Graduate Texts in Mathematics, Vol. 228)
**ISBN:** 978-0-387-27226-9

## Summary

The standard graduate textbook on modular forms, covering the prerequisite material for understanding Wiles's proof. Develops the theory from congruence subgroups through Hecke operators to the modularity theorem, with the explicit goal of making the FLT proof accessible.

## Key Topics

- **Modular curves and modular forms**: Γ₀(N), Γ₁(N), Eisenstein series, cusp forms, dimension formulas
- **Hecke operators**: T_p, diamond operators, eigenforms, newforms, Atkin-Lehner theory
- **L-functions**: Completed L-functions, functional equations, Mellin transforms
- **Galois representations**: ℓ-adic representations attached to modular forms, Eichler-Shimura relation
- **Modularity theorem**: Statement and outline of proof strategy (R = T)

## Relevance to Proof Problems

Essential prerequisite for any proof problem involving modular forms, L-functions, or the Langlands program. Provides the Hecke algebra T that appears in Wiles's R = T argument.
