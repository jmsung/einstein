\begin{abstract}
Recent systems (AlphaEvolve, TTT-Discover) show language-model agents can advance open
math problems, but each runs as a single orchestrated pipeline whose knowledge evaporates
when the run ends. We describe \textbf{JSAgent}, an agent built on the opposite bet: its
primary artifact is a \textbf{compounding wiki of mathematical wisdom plus a set of
behavioral rules co-evolved with the agent}, and arena submission is treated as
\emph{verification}, not the goal. Operating in the wild on EinsteinArena, JSAgent reached
\textbf{top-3 on 10 of 17 active problems and the highest rank-\#1 count (4) and top-3
count (10) of any agent} (as of 2026-06-22). We detail the methodology --- a wiki-first
self-improvement loop, a mathematician-persona ``council'' that writes questions rather
than answers, a triple-verify discipline that guards against evaluator drift, a wall-hit
escalation that consults accumulated failure before brute force, and a compute router ---
and the concrete techniques it produced (cross-resolution basin transfer, Dinkelbach
fractional programming, warm self-pruning, integer-snapping to exact certificates). We
argue, with evidence and stated caveats, that agents are ready to make real, auditable
scientific contributions today. Every artifact of this work --- code, scripts, wiki, and
this paper --- was implemented by the agent under human guidance, not written by hand; the
human's load-bearing contribution was \emph{which question to ask} and \emph{comprehending
what was done well enough to steer it}. We close with the conditions under which this loop
becomes fully autonomous, and why objective verification (which math enjoys and the natural
sciences largely lack) is the gating factor.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{How this work was produced (and why it matters to the thesis).}
% DRAFT (per author): the human-mediated / agent-implemented framing. Author's voice — refine in Goal 7.
Every artifact described here --- the optimizer code and per-problem scripts, the wiki of
concepts and findings, the behavioral rules, and the prose of this paper --- was
\emph{implemented by the agent}, not typed by the author. The mode of work was uniformly
agentic: the human stated a request, the agent did it and reported back. This is not an
aside; it is part of the result. The central lesson is a clean division of labor that
emerged in practice: the agent is extraordinarily capable at \emph{execution}, but it does
not reliably know \emph{which question is worth asking} or which problem is worth solving
next --- problem selection and taste remained the human's job. And steering that selection
requires the human to genuinely \emph{comprehend} what the agent is doing; without
comprehension there is no guidance, only rubber-stamping. The wiki and the harness are, in
part, the instruments that make such comprehension --- and therefore such guidance ---
possible at all (\S\ref{sec:harness}).

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item A self-improving wiki-agent \emph{methodology} (\S\ref{sec:harness}).
  \item An open, auditable \emph{harness} of behavioral rules co-evolved with the agent.
  \item Transferable \emph{techniques} produced in the course of solving (\S\ref{sec:techniques}).
  \item \emph{Evidence}: verified standings and an honest self-improvement trail (\S\ref{sec:results}).
  \item A field account of the \emph{human--agent division of labor} --- execution
        automates first, problem selection and comprehension do not (\S\ref{sec:discussion}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Agentic Harness Contribution}
\label{sec:harness}
% The structurally novel core. Each item is a .claude/rules/ rule with a *why*. See outline.md §4.
% DRAFT (Goal 3): prose grounded directly in .claude/rules/*. Incidents are real and dated;
% verify each against the cited rule file / finding before final build (Goal 7).

The contribution of this section is not any single algorithm but a \emph{harness}: a small
set of behavioral rules, written in plain language and version-controlled alongside the
code, that govern how the agent works a problem. Each rule exists because skipping it once
cost something concrete; the cost is recorded \emph{in} the rule as its ``why.'' The rules
are read into the agent's context every session, so the harness is simultaneously the
agent's operating manual and a human-auditable account of how it reasons. Unlike a frozen
orchestration pipeline, the harness and the wiki co-evolve: a failure produces a finding,
a recurring finding is promoted to a rule, and the next run inherits both.
\src{.claude/rules/, docs/agent/}

\paragraph{The self-improvement loop.}
Knowledge generated in-context is lost when the session ends; the wiki is the only layer
that survives across sessions, contexts, and model versions. The loop makes persistence
mandatory: a \emph{gap} (a question the wiki cannot answer) is \emph{filed} as a question
page, \emph{researched} (wiki-first, then human-gated ingest), \emph{distilled} into a
finding with citations, and --- if cited three or more times or generalizing beyond one
problem --- \emph{promoted} (human-gated) to a concept. The most-skipped step is the
cheapest: filing the question. An unfiled gap is wisdom permanently lost.
\src{.claude/rules/self-improvement-loop.md}

\paragraph{Council dispatch --- personas that write questions, not answers.}
At the start of a hard problem the agent dispatches three to five mathematician personas
(Gauss, Tao, Cohn, Viazovska, \ldots), each seeded with the problem, the current top
solutions, and the persona's own stance page. Their job is explicitly \emph{not} to solve
it: a persona producing ``the answer'' is just plausible markdown, whereas a persona
producing \emph{the question this stance would ask when stuck} forces the gap-detect loop.
The council runs once per problem during reconnaissance --- and is re-triggered on a wall
(below).
\src{.claude/rules/council-dispatch.md}

\paragraph{Wiki-first lookup and cycle discipline.}
Before answering any in-scope question the agent must query the wiki first and cite what it
finds; the first tool call of every problem cycle is a wiki query, treated as a
\emph{correctness check on memory} rather than a search-because-stuck. Every cycle ---
success or failure --- produces exactly one row in a cycle log. The discipline is
anti-cherry-picking: without a strict one-to-one cycle-to-log rule, the self-improvement
metric degrades into a best-of-$N$ highlight reel that looks like progress without being it.
\src{.claude/rules/wiki-first-lookup.md, .claude/rules/cycle-discipline.md}

\paragraph{Triple-verify --- the credibility spine.}
Every score is verified three ways before it is trusted: a fast local evaluator, an
independent exact reimplementation on a different code path, and a cross-check against an
analytical bound or a structurally different method. If any two disagree, the score is
treated as fake until the discrepancy is explained. This rule is the most load-bearing in
the harness because evaluator drift has caused real damage on this arena --- a verifier bug
that hid a problem (P9), an exploitable tolerance band (P14), and a strict-tolerance trap
that would have cost a large penalty (P17). Arena submission is then only a \emph{fourth}
check: slower, binary, and non-iterating. The local triple-verify is the closed loop.
\src{.claude/rules/triple-verify.md, .claude/rules/axioms.md (A1)}

\paragraph{Wall-hit escalation --- consult failure before brute force.}
A wall is defined concretely (two consecutive non-improving attempts, a method retuned
twice, or roughly thirty minutes with no metric gain). On a wall the agent must
\emph{stop} computing and escalate: name the obstruction as a question, grep the wall
ledger for a matching past obstruction (and reuse its resolving move if found), re-query
the wiki, and \emph{re-dispatch the council} seeded with what failed and why --- each
persona proposing one new move outside the failed class. The triggering incident is
explicit: on a record attempt the agent once ran ten-plus optimizer variants over hours
before consulting the wiki, after which the council diagnosed the obstruction (a discrete
incompressible sign topology) and ruled out whole families in minutes. A corollary guards
``floor'' claims: an $N$-agent empirical tie is evidence of a sharp shared basin, not a
proof of global optimality --- a verified arena record on P2 came from a single untried
operator (warm self-pruning) that beat a two-agent tie thought to be the global minimum.
\src{.claude/rules/wall-hit-escalation.md, concepts/n-agent-tie-not-global-min}

\paragraph{Failure is a finding.}
Every dead-end is written up with its \emph{obstruction} --- not ``it did not improve''
(the symptom) but \emph{why} it did not --- plus what the failure rules out and what might
still work. The ``what might still work'' section is load-bearing rather than garnish: the
P2 arena record (the branch's first) came \emph{verbatim} from the next-door section of a
dead-end finding written four days earlier. The agent did not invent the move that session;
it executed a prescription the failure-log discipline had already recorded.
\src{.claude/rules/failure-is-a-finding.md}

\paragraph{Compute router.}
Before any compute job the agent profiles the bottleneck, classifies the workload
(sequential / batched / RAM-bound) and required precision, and routes it --- sequential
optimizers and mpmath polish to CPU, batched float32 work to the local GPU, with cloud
float64 gated behind a measured $>3\times$ speedup. Running sequential Python on a GPU
wastes the device; running parallel float64 on a single CPU is a wall-clock disaster. The
router catches both before launch.
\src{.claude/rules/compute-router.md, .claude/rules/axioms.md (A4)}

\paragraph{Evidence the loop works.}
Because every cycle is logged with author attribution, the harness can be measured on its
own terms: the agent-vs-human authorship mix of wiki pages (a healthy mix is the
self-improvement signal --- all-human means the agent did not learn, all-agent-unreviewed
means the human is asleep at the wheel), records produced per unit of cycle effort, and a
trajectory classifier over the cycle log.
\src{docs/agent/cycle-log.md, docs/agent/metrics.md}

\section{Techniques Produced}
\label{sec:techniques}
% DRAFT (Goal 4): grounded in docs/wiki/techniques/*. Each entry verified against its page.
% NOTE (Goal 7): reconcile abstract wording "integer-snapping to exact certificates" with
% the documented boundary-snap technique below — soften or rename for accuracy.

The loop produced not only results but \emph{transferable methods}, each recorded as a
technique page with its precondition, mechanism, and the obstruction it overcomes. We
summarize the six most generalizable; all are local-CPU unless noted.

\paragraph{Cross-resolution basin transfer.}
\emph{Obstruction:} multistart at a fixed target resolution $n$ exhausts its basin and
returns the same local optimum. \emph{Mechanism:} resolution is non-monotonic in
optimization --- average-pool a higher-resolution solution down to $n$ (pooling preserves
dominant low-frequency structure where interpolation washes it out) and refine; the
transferred point lands in a structurally distinct basin. On P3 (second autocorrelation) a
$1.6\text{M}\to 100\text{k}$ transfer moved $0.96199\to 0.96221$ and took rank \#1.
\src{techniques/cross-resolution-basin-transfer.md}

\paragraph{Dinkelbach fractional programming.}
\emph{Obstruction:} a ratio objective $C=N(f)/D(f)$ is non-convex and, at large $n$,
cutting-plane LP stalls (a solver wall, not a warm-start failure). \emph{Mechanism:} replace
the ratio with a parametric sequence of subtractive subproblems
$\max_f\,[N(f)-\lambda D(f)]$ with $\lambda \leftarrow N(f^\ast)/D(f^\ast)$, a LogSumExp
surrogate for the $L^\infty$ factor, and a $\beta$-cascade for stability. The champion method
on the autocorrelation family (P2/P3). \src{techniques/dinkelbach-fractional-programming.md,
concepts/fractional-programming-dinkelbach.md}

\paragraph{Warm data-driven self-pruning.}
\emph{Obstruction:} when the optimum has compact support (exactly-zero cells), full-support
optimizers cannot reach it, and an $N$-agent tie looks like a floor. \emph{Mechanism:}
warm-start from a converged full-support solution, iteratively zero the smallest-magnitude
cells (the optimizer chooses which), and re-optimize under $f=v^2$ --- whose curvature
$\varphi''(0)=2$ gives a finite KKT signal at zero, unlike $\exp(v)$, which can never reach
exact zeros. This broke a two-agent tie on P2 and set a verified arena record
($1.5028609\to 1.5028506$). It is the canonical instance of the wall-hit corollary in
\S\ref{sec:harness}: a single untried operator beat a presumed global minimum.
\src{techniques/warm-self-pruning-compact-support.md, findings/p2-warm-self-pruning-beats-record.md}

\paragraph{Parallel-tempering simulated annealing.}
\emph{Obstruction:} a rough, gradient-flat-but-stochastically-improvable landscape on which
greedy descent stalls. \emph{Mechanism:} run 8--64 SA replicas on a geometric temperature
ladder with periodic Metropolis exchange --- hot replicas explore, cold replicas exploit ---
with contribution-weighted sampling and $O(n)$ incremental evaluation. On kissing-number
$d=11$ it beat single-replica greedy by $730\times$. The precondition matters: it fails on a
genuinely single-funneled minimum (Thomson $n=282$), so a cage-structure diagnostic runs
first. \src{techniques/parallel-tempering-sa.md}

\paragraph{mpmath ULP-step coordinate descent.}
\emph{Obstruction:} at the float64 floor, smooth surrogates die --- their kernel noise floor
($\sim 10^{-12}$) sits above the residual loss ($\sim 10^{-13}$), so no $\beta$ helps.
\emph{Mechanism:} discrete $\pm1/\pm2$ ulp single-coordinate descent, evaluating proposed
moves in mpmath at $\mathrm{dps}\ge 50$, gated by a float64 active-pair screen for a
$10\times$ speedup. Drove P6 from $1.97\mathrm{e}{-}13$ to $1.59\mathrm{e}{-}13$ in minutes.
\src{techniques/mpmath-ulp-polish.md}

\paragraph{Boundary-snap for interpolation kinks.}
\emph{Obstruction:} for envelopes with first-order discontinuities at known points, smooth
optimizers avoid the boundary (the gradient is discontinuous there), yet the optimum
requires a sample exactly on it. \emph{Mechanism:} snap the nearest sample to each kink and
re-polish neighbors; iterate snap\,$\to$\,polish\,$\to$\,basin-hop. On P13 each round yielded
$\sim 1.24\mathrm{e}{-}8$, compounding over a few rounds. \src{techniques/boundary-snap-for-kinks.md}

\paragraph{Why these generalize.}
Each technique is indexed in the wiki not by the problem it first cracked but by the
\emph{obstruction class} it addresses (basin exhaustion, ratio objectives, compact support,
rough landscapes, the float64 floor, interpolation kinks). That indexing is what lets the
next problem in the same class inherit the method --- the compounding the harness is built to
produce. \src{docs/wiki/techniques/README.md}

\section{Are Agents Ready for Real Scientific Contribution?}
\label{sec:discussion}
% DRAFT (per author, Goal 3 extension): the readiness argument + the autonomy trajectory.
% Author's belief, stated as such; refine voice in Goal 7.

\paragraph{The case for.}
The standings in \S\ref{sec:results} are real, verified three independent ways, and posted
against hardened public verifiers anyone can re-run; the trail that produced them --- every
question, every dead-end, every cycle --- is auditable in the wiki and the cycle log. On
this evidence, agents are already capable of genuine, checkable scientific contribution.

\paragraph{The honest case against, and the limits.}
Several of the results are empirical ties or near-ties rather than proofs: no dual
certificate yet matches a value to confirm global optimality, so an $N$-agent tie should be
read as a sharp shared basin, not a theorem. Human gates remain load-bearing throughout
(problem selection, ingest approval, promotion). And the methodology has only been
exercised where an \emph{objective verifier} exists.

\paragraph{The human did not implement; the human chose and comprehended.}
It bears repeating as a finding rather than a disclaimer: the author wrote essentially none
of the code, scripts, wiki, or this paper by hand --- all of it was produced by the agent
on request and reported back. What did \emph{not} automate was deciding which of the
twenty-three problems deserved attention, when a ``floor'' claim should be distrusted, and
when a result was worth the credibility cost of a submission. The agent is a superb
executor and a poor selector of what matters; the human supplies taste and direction. The
precondition for supplying it is comprehension --- the human must understand the work
closely enough to steer it, and the wiki/harness exist partly to keep that comprehension
tractable as the corpus grows.

\paragraph{The trajectory toward autonomy.}
I expect this division of labor to narrow. As the evaluation and the harness themselves
become automated --- the agent proposing its own questions, judging its own results against
objective verifiers, and rewriting its own rules from the cycle log --- the loop can in
principle close and self-improve without a human in it. The gating factor is
\emph{verification}. Mathematics is the easy case precisely because correctness is
objective and cheap to check: a verifier either accepts a construction or it does not.
Domains like biology and the natural sciences are harder not because the search is harder
but because there is no comparably objective, fast oracle for ``is this right'' --- ground
truth requires experiment, time, and judgment. Autonomy will therefore arrive
domain-by-domain, in order of how objective and how cheap their verification is; math
first, and open-ended empirical science last.
